About this test

The twenty-meter shuttle run is a maximal aerobic-fitness assessment. Athletes run between two lines twenty meters apart at a pace that increases each minute. The test ends when an athlete can no longer keep up with the prescribed pace.

How the test works

You'll run back and forth between two lines — set twenty meters apart.

Stay in your lane. Each time you hear a beep, you should be at a line. Place your foot on, or over the line, pivot, and run to the other side. Don't make wide turns — they cost you time.

The pace starts slow and gets faster every minute.

When you can't reach the line in time on two beeps in a row, you're done. Leave the testing area. And remember your final lap count — the big number on the screen — repeat it to yourself a few times so you don't forget. That number is your score.

After you finish, walk for a few minutes to cool down. Don't sit down right away.

When you're ready, line up at the start line — and play the test.

Protocol

Léger, L. A. & Lambert, J. (1982). A maximal multistage 20-m shuttle run test to predict VO2 max. European Journal of Applied Physiology, 49(1): 1–12.

Implementation

Twenty-one progressive levels, two-hundred-forty-four total shuttles, ~21 minutes maximum duration. Beep timings derived from the published Léger formula (shuttle duration = 72 ÷ speed in km/h).
